# Customer Avatar: The Confused Cleanser

## Demographics

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 24–40 |
| **Gender** | 70% female, 30% male |
| **Income** | $40K–$90K |
| **Location** | US/UK/AU, suburban or urban |
| **Education** | Some college to college-educated |

## Platform Behavior

| Platform | Role |
|---|---|
| **TikTok** | Primary discovery. Where they first hear about cleanses. Comments are where confusion lives |
| **Instagram** | Secondary. Product discovery via Reels and Stories |
| **YouTube** | Deep learning. Watches 20-45 min protocol breakdowns |
| **Reddit** | Validation. Checks r/parasites, r/supplements, r/alternativehealth for "did this work?" |
| **Google** | Last resort. Searches "parasite cleanse day by day schedule" when overwhelmed |

## Psychographic Profile

### State of Mind
Already decided to do a cleanse. NOT on the fence. Overwhelmed by HOW to execute, not WHETHER to start.

### Trigger Event
Saw a parasite/gut cleanse TikTok, went down a 2-hour rabbit hole, now has 8 browser tabs open, 3 supplement carts half-built, and no coherent plan.

### Core Pain (in their words)
- "I bought 6 supplements from 3 different TikToks and I don't know when to take any of them"
- "Am I supposed to take the binder before or after the herbs?"
- "Is this die-off or am I actually getting sick?"
- "How long am I supposed to do this?"
- "What do I eat during this?"

### Core Desire
Structure, not more information. "Just tell me what to do today."

### Budget Reality
Already spending $50–150 on supplements. $79 for an app that organizes the protocol into a daily system = easy yes. Price is not the barrier — understanding what the product does is.

### Trust Signals (ranked)
1. Real product screenshots showing the actual app in use
2. Specificity — exact products, exact timing, exact days
3. Completion proof — "I finished the 21 days" > "I feel amazing"
4. Anti-hype tone — distrust of "wellness" marketing language

### Fears
- Die-off symptoms on Day 3-5 meaning something is wrong
- Doing the protocol wrong and wasting money
- Taking supplements that interact badly
- Being scammed by another wellness product

### Buying Behavior
- Impulse-buy from TikTok/IG links
- Decides in <60 seconds
- Needs clarity, not persuasion
- Will abandon cart if landing page feels like generic wellness
- Responds to "I get it immediately" product demos

## Jobs To Be Done

| Job | Priority |
|---|---|
| "Tell me what to take and when" | #1 — this is the purchase trigger |
| "Show me what to buy before I start" | #2 — prep friction kills activation |
| "Tell me if what I'm feeling is normal" | #3 — die-off anxiety causes dropoff |
| "Keep me on track across 21 days" | #4 — completion = success metric |
| "Help me when I'm stuck without breaking my flow" | #5 — contextual AI, not chatbot |

## What They Are NOT

- They are NOT looking for an app — they're looking for answers to comment-section questions
- They are NOT health hobbyists — they have a specific protocol they want to complete
- They are NOT price-sensitive — they're already buying supplements
- They are NOT seeking medical diagnosis — they want execution support

## How This Informs Marketing

### Content should meet them WHERE THEY ARE
In TikTok comments, confused, with 6 supplements on their counter. Our content answers the exact question they just typed.

### Landing page should feel like RELIEF
Not "another wellness product" — but "oh, someone organized this for me."

### The emotional close
> "I finally have a system that makes this protocol feel doable."
