# Landing Page Repositioning Brief

## Objective

Redo the landing page so cold traffic understands the product in seconds, feels the relief of having a clear protocol system, and wants to start immediately.

This page should not sell "wellness content" or "AI." It should sell a concrete result:

**The Gut Brain Journal turns a dense 21-day gut reset into a clear daily app you can actually follow.**

## What The Current Landing Page Misses

The current page is directionally cleaner than a typical hype-heavy wellness page, but it still undersells the actual product.

Primary gaps:

- It describes the product abstractly instead of showing the workflow in use.
- It says "guided protocol companion," which is accurate but too soft for cold traffic.
- It spends visual energy on atmosphere before proving what the app does.
- It does not create the "I get it immediately" moment for someone who arrives from social media.
- It has no strong real-use proof block. The app itself is the proof, but the landing page is not exposing it.

## Recommended Product-Market-Fit Angle

### Core Positioning

Do not position this as:

- an AI wellness app
- a health platform
- a self-optimization dashboard
- a generic journal

Position it as:

**A guided 21-day gut reset app that tells you what to buy, what to do today, and what to ask when you get stuck.**

### The Real Job To Be Done

The buyer is not mainly purchasing information.

They are purchasing:

- relief from confusion
- confidence that they are doing the protocol correctly
- momentum through a hard 21-day commitment
- a cleaner alternative to juggling a PDF, notes app, shopping list, and chatbot

### Emotional Promise

The strongest emotional hook is not "better gut health" on the page by itself.

It is:

**"I finally have a system that makes this protocol feel doable."**

That is the butterfly feeling. The landing page should make the protocol feel simple, organized, and in motion.

## Recommended Headline Direction

### Recommended Hero

**A 21-day gut reset app you can actually follow.**

Subhead:

**See what to buy, what to do today, and where to get help without digging through a PDF or guessing your way through the protocol.**

Support line options:

- No PDF chaos. No protocol guesswork. No coach required.
- Built for people who want structure, not more research.
- Open the app and know the next move immediately.

### Backup Hero Directions

Option 2:

**Stop trying to run your reset out of a PDF.**

Subhead:

**The Gut Brain Journal turns your 21-day protocol into a day-by-day app with checklists, shopping guidance, and in-context help.**

Option 3:

**Your gut reset, organized like software.**

Subhead:

**Prep once, follow today, ask when stuck, and finish the protocol with less confusion and more momentum.**

## The 3 Selling Points To Lead With

These should drive the homepage above the fold and in the first scroll.

### 1. It tells me exactly what to do today

This is the strongest value prop.

Why it wins:

- instantly legible
- low skepticism
- high relief
- directly tied to app behavior

### 2. It organizes the entire protocol before I start

This is the second strongest value prop because prep friction kills activation.

Why it wins:

- lowers overwhelm before Day 1
- makes purchase feel practical
- gives immediate utility even before the reset starts

### 3. It gives me help in the moment without breaking the flow

This is where AI belongs in the message.

Why it wins:

- specific enough to be credible
- valuable without sounding like fake medical intelligence
- supports the core promise instead of replacing it

## What To De-Emphasize

- broad "transformation" language unless tied to real customer proof
- symptom tracking as a headline feature
- mood logging as a hero feature
- generic AI framing
- decorative motion that does not clarify the product

These are support features, not the reason cold traffic converts.

## Homepage Structure

### 1. Hero: Clarity + Immediate Proof

Left side:

- direct headline
- one-sentence subhead
- primary CTA
- 3 short proof chips:
  - Know what to buy
  - Know what to do today
  - Ask when stuck

Right side:

- one polished autoplay desktop product loop
- not a fake mockup
- real app interaction footage

The hero should answer in under 5 seconds:

- what this is
- who it is for
- why it is better than a PDF

### 2. Three Autoplay Demo Sections

Each section should pair:

- a muted looping product video
- one strong headline
- one short explanation
- a "before vs after" framing line

### Demo 1: Prep Day / Shopping

Headline:

**Start the reset without chaos.**

Show:

- opening Prep Day
- scanning the shopping list
- checking off purchase items
- seeing which phase items are "shop now" versus later

What this communicates:

- this is a real system, not content
- the product lowers startup friction immediately
- the protocol feels organized before it begins

### Demo 2: Today's Plan / Checklist / Ask AI

Headline:

**Open the app and see exactly what today looks like.**

Show:

- today's checklist grouped by morning, afternoon, evening
- tapping a step
- opening the AI explanation
- checking items off

What this communicates:

- daily clarity
- visible progress
- contextual help

This is likely the highest-converting demo.

### Demo 3: Move Through The Protocol

Headline:

**The plan evolves as the protocol evolves.**

Show:

- moving from Prep Day to later days
- phase changes in the top bar
- logging symptoms or notes
- reopening the guide or shopping reference without losing the workflow

What this communicates:

- this is not a static checklist
- the app supports the full 21-day journey
- the experience has structure and momentum over time

### 3. "Why This Converts Better Than A PDF" Section

This should be a plain-language comparison block.

Example contrast:

- PDF: long, static, easy to ignore
- Random notes app: fragmented
- Generic chatbot: no protocol context
- Gut Brain Journal: one place for prep, today, and guidance

This is strong because the enemy is obvious and familiar.

### 4. Real Proof Section

Best version:

- real customer quote
- short result summary
- photo or simple identity marker if available

If real customer proof is not ready yet:

- use real product-in-use proof instead of fake transformation copy
- say less, but say only what is real

Do not invent testimonials.

### 5. Pricing + CTA

The pricing section should feel like a low-friction decision:

- one-time purchase
- immediate access
- simple scope
- clear who it is for

Potential support copy:

**For people who want a clear day-by-day reset system, not another pile of wellness information.**

## Recording Plan For The Website Videos

These should look like premium product footage, not random screen recordings.

### Capture Rules

- record the real desktop app
- use a clean seeded account with believable progress and entries
- hide distractions
- use smooth cursor movement
- keep each loop around 8 to 14 seconds
- no audio needed
- export as lightweight `webm` and `mp4`
- include a poster frame for fast page load

### Recording Setup

- desktop viewport around `1440x900`
- use real-looking journal entries and checklist states
- keep the interface populated so nothing looks empty
- use subtle zoom or crop in editing if needed
- avoid frantic cursor motion

### Suggested Scripts

#### Video 1: Prep Day

1. Open Prep Day.
2. Scroll a little through the shopping list.
3. Check off 2-3 items.
4. Click "Simplify this list" or "Budget version."
5. End on a clean organized state.

#### Video 2: Today Flow

1. Show today's checklist.
2. Tap a supplement or meal step.
3. Let the AI response appear.
4. Check the item off.
5. Check one more item so visible progress increases.

#### Video 3: Across Days

1. Use Next day to move forward.
2. Show the phase/day indicator changing.
3. Open guide or shopping reference.
4. Log a symptom or note.
5. Return to the main workflow.

## Motion / Visual Direction

The videos should be the main showpiece.

That means the header animation should support them, not compete with them.

### Recommendation

Do not lead with a heavy 3D concept unless it directly improves understanding.

Better options:

- a refined parallax dashboard composition
- a subtle protocol timeline animation
- a restrained progress-line or orbit animation behind the hero
- soft layered motion that frames the app footage

Bad outcome to avoid:

- the page feels "cool"
- but the visitor still cannot tell what the product actually does

## Copy Rules

- Use direct language.
- Prefer "app," "plan," "today," and "check off" over vague wellness terms.
- Show the workflow before the philosophy.
- Sell relief and structure before aspiration.
- Keep AI in a support role.
- Avoid medical or outcome claims that are not operationally defensible.

## My Recommendation

If the goal is conversion from cold traffic, the homepage should center on this message:

**This is the app that makes a 21-day gut reset feel clear, organized, and doable every day.**

The fastest path is:

1. Replace the abstract hero with direct positioning and one real autoplay product loop.
2. Add three product-in-use demo sections.
3. Reframe the page around "prep, today, ask when stuck."
4. Reduce decorative motion unless it sharpens the product story.
