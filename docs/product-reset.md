# Product Reset Brief

## Product
The Gut Brain Journal is a guided protocol companion for people who want a clear 21-day gut reset plan without turning a PDF, notes app, and chatbot into their own system.

## Vision
Turn dense wellness protocols into focused mini web apps people can actually finish.

## Mission
Help overwhelmed users know exactly what to do today, why it matters, and what to log as they move through a 21-day gut reset.

## Target User
- Adults dealing with recurring gut symptoms who are motivated to self-manage.
- People who want structure and momentum more than endless education.
- Buyers looking for a low-friction, self-serve product, not a diagnostic tool or high-touch coaching program.

## Job To Be Done
When I start a gut reset, help me follow the plan day by day so I do not get lost, skip steps, or fall back into research paralysis.

## Product Principles
1. Protocol first, AI second.
2. Every screen should reduce confusion.
3. The app should answer "what do I do today?" before anything else.
4. Personalization should clarify the plan, not create a new one.
5. Trust beats hype. Avoid claims that are not proven or operationally real.
6. Completion is the primary success metric.

## Core User Flow
1. Prep: understand the protocol, gather supplies, and shop for the next phase.
2. Today: complete the daily plan in order.
3. Log: capture symptoms, mood, and notes.
4. Learn More: ask for clarification only when the user is stuck.

## Feature Scope

| Keep and emphasize | Keep but de-emphasize | Cut from marketing/core path |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daily plan | Progress settings | Deep insights as a headline feature |
| Prep and shopping flow | Export/clear journal utilities | Platform-style analytics framing |
| Phase guide/reference | Mood tracking | Community/support promises unless real |
| Step-by-step checklist | Symptom logging | Broad "AI health platform" positioning |
| AI step explanation and troubleshooting | Historical charts | Fake urgency, inflated stats, unsupported testimonials |

## Success Metrics
- Activation: user completes Prep Day.
- Adherence: user completes Day 1 and Day 3.
- Completion: checklist completion rate by Day 7 and Day 21.
- Confidence: user reports they know what to do next.
- Outcome: self-reported symptom change at Day 7 and Day 21.
- Conversion: landing visit to paid start.

## Near-Term Execution
1. Rewrite positioning around a guided protocol companion.
2. Simplify the in-app information architecture to prep, today, and guide.
3. Remove low-trust marketing claims and non-core promises.
4. Validate the narrowed product with target users before expanding features again.
